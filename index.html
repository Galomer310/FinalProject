Tribute page To Nikola Tesla

Biography

Early Life

and Family: Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, in the Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia). He was one of five children. His father, Milutin Tesla, was a Serbian Orthodox priest and writer, and his mother, Georgina Đuka Tesla, had a talent for making home craft tools and mechanical appliances, an early influence on Tesla's inventive mind.

Education: Tesla showed an early aptitude for mathematics and engineering. He attended the Technical University of Graz, Austria, and later studied at the University of Prague. Although he did not complete a degree, his studies provided a strong foundation in physics and mathematics.

Career Beginnings

Early Work: Tesla worked in the telephony and electrical engineering fields before emigrating to the United States in 1884. He initially worked for Thomas Edison, but the two had a falling out due to differing opinions on electricity (AC vs. DC).

AC vs. DC: Tesla became a staunch advocate of alternating current (AC) systems, which he believed were more efficient and practical than Edison's direct current (DC) systems.

Major Contributions and Inventions

Tesla Coil: Invented in 1891, the Tesla Coil is a high-frequency transformer that produces high-voltage, low-current electricity. It is used in radio technology, wireless transmission experiments, and educational demonstrations.

Induction Motor and Polyphase AC: Tesla developed the induction motor and polyphase AC systems, which were critical for the widespread adoption of AC power. These inventions formed the basis of modern electrical power distribution.

Niagara Falls Project: Tesla’s work was instrumental in the development of the hydroelectric power plant at Niagara Falls, which was the first major demonstration of AC power's viability.
